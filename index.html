I'm an Affective Computing Researcher

  • Name: Akiri Surely
  • Age: 22 Years
  • Residence: United States
  • Hometown: Nigeria

I am a highly skilled, experienced and ever learning individual, who applies much passion and ingenuity in building usable and effective systems. Desiring to be relevant, I seek and apply up-to-the minute and appropriate skills in the designs, implementation and evaluations, thereby progressively coping with the next generation of human computer interfaces. Seeing the possibility of my impacting the discipline itself, my country and the world through a “gateway” graduate program, I enrolled in the University of Maryland Baltimore County (UMBC).

I'm currently a Ph.D. student in Human-Centered Computing (HCC) Program in the Information Systems Department at UMBC. I am advised by Dr. Andrea Kleinsmith . I am a member of the Affective Behavior Interaction Lab. My primary research interests are in Affective Computing and Human-Computer Interaction and focus on measuring and modeling affective nonverbal behavior, particularly body expressions. Currently, I am conducting research to investigate correlation between body expressions and physiological signals in first responders.
